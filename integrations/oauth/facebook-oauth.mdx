This guide explains how to create a Facebook app and configure ngrok to use Facebook OAuth for user authentication.
For more detail, see the [Facebook app registration](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/apps#register) documentation.

## What you'll need

- A [Facebook Developer](https://developers.facebook.com/apps/) account (convert your account to a developer account if needed).
- Your ngrok authtoken and an endpoint with the OAuth action in its Traffic Policy.

## Register an application

1. Visit the [App Dashboard](https://developers.facebook.com/apps/), log in, and convert your account to a developer account if necessary.
2. Select **Create app**.
3. Enter a user-visible app name and contact email.
4. When prompted to select a use case, choose **Authenticate and request data from users with Facebook Login**.
5. When prompted to connect a business portfolio, you can skip this step. It isn't required to authenticate users to your own service.
6. Finish creating your app.

<Note>
The **Authenticate and request data from users with Facebook Login** use case sets up standard [Facebook Login](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/facebook-login/), which lets visitors sign in with their personal Facebook account. This is the right fit for gating a service behind an ngrok endpoint.
You don't need [Facebook Login for Business](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/facebook-login/facebook-login-for-business) or the [Business app type](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/development/create-an-app/app-dashboard/app-types/) unless your app needs ongoing access to business assets such as Pages or ad accounts.

Connecting a [business portfolio](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/development/create-an-app/) is optional during creation and can be done later.
</Note>

## Configure your application

1. From your app dashboard, select **Use cases** in the left-hand navigation.
2. Select **Customize** (the pencil icon) for the **Authenticate and request data from users with Facebook Login** use case.
3. Open the use case's **Settings** and, in **Valid OAuth Redirect URIs**, add `https://idp.ngrok.com/oauth2/callback`.
4. Save the settings.
5. In the left-hand navigation, select **App settings** and then **Advanced**.
6. Fill out additional settings for your application.
   - ngrok does not support Server IP allowlisting.
7. Enable **Require App Secret**.
   See [Facebook's documentation](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/facebook-login/security/#appsecret) for how to call the Graph API with this feature.
8. Save settings.
9. In the left-hand navigation, open **App settings** and then **Basic**.
10. At the top, note your **App ID** and **App Secret** for later.
11. Fill out the privacy policy URL.
   This URL must be accessible when entered for verification.
12. Select a category for your application.
13. Click **Save Changes**.
14. Switch your app from **Development** to **Live** mode using the toggle at the top of the page, and confirm.
15. Your application should now show as live.

## User permission revocation

Facebook allows revocation of any permission as part of the authorization flow.
ngrok enforces that users initially grant all configured permissions.
After endpoint authorization, users may selectively revoke permissions.
If your application requires more than the `default` or `email` scope, follow [Facebook's rules](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/facebook-login/handling-declined-permissions#reprompt) for handling revoked permissions without violating terms of use.
