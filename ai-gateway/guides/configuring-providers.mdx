title: Configuring Providers
description: Manage built-in and custom AI providers in app.ngrok.ai.
---

You can manage **built-in** and **custom** providers in [app.ngrok.ai](https://app.ngrok.ai). Account-wide timeouts and token limits live in [Account Settings](/ai-gateway/guides/account-settings)—not on individual providers.

## Built-in providers

Built-in providers are catalog providers—OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Groq, and others listed in the [model catalog](/ai-gateway/reference/model-catalog).

- **OpenAI and Anthropic**: use ngrok.ai inference with [credits](/ai-gateway/concepts/credits), or bring your own key via an [access key configuration](/ai-gateway/guides/access-key-configurations)
- **Other built-in providers**: require a stored provider key attached in a configuration

See [Providers](/ai-gateway/concepts/providers) for an overview.

## Custom providers

Custom providers are endpoints you define, such as Ollama, vLLM, private APIs, and other OpenAI- or Anthropic-compatible services.

To register one, see [Create a custom provider](/ai-gateway/concepts/custom-providers#create-a-custom-provider) or the [Providers API](/api-reference/providers/create).

## Routing traffic to providers

Storing a provider (built-in or custom) does not route traffic by itself. For each [access key](/ai-gateway/concepts/api-keys):

1. Create an [access key configuration](/ai-gateway/guides/access-key-configurations) with the right access scope and routing rules.
2. Assign the configuration to the key.
3. Send requests with the access key and a `model` (or `provider:model`) the configuration allows.

Store upstream credentials with [Provider Keys](/ai-gateway/guides/attaching-provider-keys).
