ngrok lets trusted third-party cloud services like GitHub, Twilio, and Stripe send webhooks to applications running behind your firewall. We verify webhook signatures at our network edge, so only legitimate traffic reaches your applications.

## Why route webhooks this way?

- **Centralize control.** Verification, routing, and secrets live in a single traffic policy. Adding a webhook provider means updating one policy, not rolling out code changes in multiple services.
- **Prevent webhook spoofing.** Signatures are verified at the edge for [70+ providers](/traffic-policy/actions/verify-webhook/#supported-providers). Spoofed or tampered requests are rejected before they reach any of your compute.
